# Downhill Madness — M1 Marketing Materials

## Roblox Game Page Description

**Title:** Downhill Madness

**Description:**

Strap in, floor it, and pray your car holds together.

**Downhill Madness** is a chaotic downhill racing game where physics is your best friend and your worst enemy. Choose from unique vehicles — from the glass-cannon Speedster to the unstoppable Tank — and barrel down procedurally built hills packed with obstacles, jumps, and terrible life choices.

**Features:**
- Three distinct vehicles with different speed, handling, and durability
- Destructible car panels — lose your hood, doors, and dignity
- Physics-driven racing with raycast suspension and real collisions
- Quick matchmaking — queue up, pick a car, race in seconds
- Leaderboards and finish times for every round
- Mobile, keyboard, and gamepad support
- New maps every round — no two runs are the same

Race to the bottom. Try not to explode.

**Tags:** Racing, Physics, Cars, Multiplayer, Destruction, Funny, Competitive

## DevForum Post — Game Announcement / Playtester Recruitment

**Title:** Downhill Madness — Physics-Driven Downhill Racing (Playtesters Wanted!)

**Category:** Cool Creations / Creations Feedback

---

Hey everyone!

We've been building **Downhill Madness**, a multiplayer downhill racing game where you pick a vehicle and barrel down procedurally generated hills. It's physics-driven, chaotic, and your car will probably lose a few doors along the way.

### What is it?

You join a server, queue up, pick from one of three vehicles (Speedster, Tank, or All-Rounder), and race to the bottom of a generated hill in under 2 minutes. Fastest time wins. If your car survives.

### Key features (M1)

- **3 vehicles** with meaningfully different stats — speed vs. durability vs. balance
- **Destructible body panels** — hood, doors, windshield, and roof all take damage
- **Raycast suspension chassis** — no jittery wheels, no VehicleSeat physics fighting
- **Procedural maps** — every round generates a fresh hill with obstacles
- **Cross-platform** — keyboard, mobile touch controls, and gamepad all supported
- **Quick lobby flow** — hub screen → queue → vehicle select → 3-2-1-GO → race → results → repeat

### Technical notes for nerds

The chassis is fully client-driven with a raycast spring-damper model. No VehicleSeat, no spring constraints, no welds on visual wheels. Surface friction varies by material (ice is terrifying). Vehicles use a modular BodyPanels system with per-panel health for future damage progression.

### What we're looking for

- General feel — is the driving fun?
- Vehicle balance — does any car feel too strong or too weak?
- Mobile experience — do touch controls feel responsive?
- Bugs — anything weird, broken, or exploding that shouldn't be
- Ideas — what would make this game worth coming back to?

Drop feedback here or in-game. We're actively developing and your input shapes what gets built next.

Thanks for checking it out. See you at the bottom of the hill.
