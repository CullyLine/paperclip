## Tone Guide

*Whimsical, gentle, full of quiet wonder. Think nature documentary narrated by an enthusiastic nine-year-old who really, really loves the ocean. Creatures have personalities. The sea is mysterious but never threatening. Every discovery feels like meeting a new friend.*

---

## Sunny Shore — Common Creatures (8)

---

### 1. Pebble Crab
**Rarity**: Common
**Tagline**: *"It's basically just a rock that decided to have a good day."*

Pebble Crabs have been collecting smooth stones since long before anyone was watching. Scientists believe they do this to look heavier than they actually are, which is an extremely good strategy and no one can say otherwise. Your Pebble Crab will sort its pebble pile by color every single morning, whether you asked it to or not.

---

### 2. Wobble Perch
**Rarity**: Common
**Tagline**: *"It wiggles when it swims. That's it. That's the whole thing. It's perfect."*

The Wobble Perch is not built for speed, dignity, or going in a straight line. It is built for wobbling, and it excels at this with tremendous enthusiasm. Marine biologists have spent years trying to explain why it lists 15 degrees to the left while swimming. The Wobble Perch has no comment at this time.

---

### 3. Sunbeam Snail
**Rarity**: Common
**Tagline**: *"Arrives late to everything, but always brings good vibes."*

Named for the way its shell catches morning light and throws tiny gold coins across the sand, the Sunbeam Snail is in absolutely no hurry. It has places to be, technically, but the exact timing is flexible. Place it near your aquarium's light source and watch it slowly, contentedly, turn to face the warmth like a tiny philosopher.

---

### 4. Frilly Flounder
**Rarity**: Common
**Tagline**: *"Flat fish. Big feelings."*

The Frilly Flounder has both of its eyes on the same side of its face and has made complete peace with this. What it lacks in symmetry, it makes up for in frilled fins that ruffle like a fancy tablecloth whenever it swims. It has strong opinions about where it sleeps in your tank and will rearrange the sand until it gets things just right.

---

### 5. Tidebell Jellyfish
**Rarity**: Common
**Tagline**: *"Drifts wherever the current takes it, and is having an incredible time."*

The Tidebell Jellyfish glows a soft, pulsing blue and makes a sound like distant wind chimes — at least, it seems like it should. No one has proven it doesn't. It has no brain, no bones, and no plans for the afternoon, and it considers all three of these things to be significant personal achievements. A fantastic aquarium addition for anyone who believes in going with the flow.

---

### 6. Button Urchin
**Rarity**: Common
**Tagline**: *"Round, spiky, and surprisingly opinionated about personal space."*

Button Urchins look like tiny purple pincushions that lost their pins and are still upset about it. They roll across the tank floor using their many little tube feet, and have been observed forming small clusters that absolutely look like a group meeting but are probably just coincidence. Probably. Feeds on algae and seems genuinely pleased about it.

---

### 7. Whisker Shrimp
**Rarity**: Common
**Tagline**: *"Has more antennae than strictly necessary, and knows it."*

The Whisker Shrimp uses its impressive antennae array to detect water currents, locate food, and appear to be listening very carefully to everything you say. It cleans the tank with cheerful efficiency, and other creatures seem to appreciate the effort, even if they don't say so. Translucent body with faint orange spots — very cute, somehow also very professional.

---

### 8. Marbled Goby
**Rarity**: Common
**Tagline**: *"The world's most patient fish. It just sits there, and it's great."*

The Marbled Goby picks a spot on a rock, sits on it, and watches. That's mostly what it does. If you watch closely, its little pectoral fins make occasional tiny adjustments, like it's very subtly applauding. Its marbled brown-and-cream pattern makes it an expert at sitting still and looking like part of the environment, which it considers a life skill of the highest order.

## Sunny Shore — Uncommon Creatures (7)

---

### 9. Glassback Hermit
**Rarity**: Uncommon
**Tagline**: *"Carries its house on its back and has decorated the inside very nicely."*

Unlike its cousins who borrow plain spiral shells, the Glassback Hermit has found a shell with a near-transparent section that lets you peek at its carefully arranged interior. It collects tiny colorful fragments — bits of sea glass, fragments of other shells, a single perfect round pebble — and arranges them inside its home. Rearranging another creature's belongings is not recommended.

---

### 10. Copper Blenny
**Rarity**: Uncommon
**Tagline**: *"Extremely nosy. Always first to investigate anything new in the tank."*

The Copper Blenny has a face like a cheerful little bulldog and uses it to investigate everything. New decoration? Copper Blenny is already inside it. Another creature acting strange? Copper Blenny is watching from behind the coral, taking mental notes. Its distinctive copper-brown coloring with neon blue eye spots make it easy to spot — which it seems to find slightly unfair.

---

### 11. Ribbonweed Eel
**Rarity**: Uncommon
**Tagline**: *"Long, ribbon-y, and absolutely full of slow mysterious energy."*

The Ribbonweed Eel moves through the water the way a ribbon moves in a breeze: graceful, unhurried, and somehow meaningful. It peeks out from behind tank decorations with one big eye and gives you a look that implies it knows something interesting. It doesn't cause any trouble. It just sort of *suggests* that it could, and that's enough.

---

### 12. Stargrazer
**Rarity**: Uncommon
**Tagline**: *"Stands on five feet and looks up at things. Relatable, honestly."*

The Stargrazer is a seastar that moves much faster than seastars have any right to move, which surprises every player who discovers one. It uses its flexible arms to prop itself upright and peer over the edge of rocks, and seems genuinely curious about what's on the other side. Soft orange-and-yellow coloration with tiny white specks — like it swallowed a handful of stars and they're slowly coming out.

---

### 13. Lace Nudibranch
**Rarity**: Uncommon
**Tagline**: *"Possibly the most beautiful thing in the entire tide pool. It knows."*

Nudibranchs are sea slugs that evolved to be absolutely breathtaking instead of hiding, and the Lace Nudibranch represents this philosophy at its peak. White body, feathery teal cerata, delicate orange trim — it moves slowly and deliberately across surfaces, aware that it is extremely worth looking at. The discovery card picture alone is worth the trip to the shore.

---

### 14. Bubble Cuttlefish
**Rarity**: Uncommon
**Tagline**: *"Changes color constantly. Currently it's your favorite color."*

The Bubble Cuttlefish can change its skin pattern in milliseconds — zebra stripes, polka dots, rippling waves, deep blue — just to express its mood, which is mostly curiosity with occasional flickers of theatrical drama. It has eight arms and two feeding tentacles, and it uses several of them to gesture enthusiastically when it sees something interesting. Very smart. Knows it.

---

### 15. Patchwork Cowrie
**Rarity**: Uncommon
**Tagline**: *"Has an extremely glossy shell and absolutely will let you know it."*

The Patchwork Cowrie maintains a shell so perfectly polished it catches light from every angle and makes the whole tank look slightly more expensive. It moves slowly along tank surfaces and keeps its shell clean by extending its colorful mantle over it — a built-in cleaning cloth. Pattern varies from creature to creature, making each one unique. Collectable? Yes. Collectible? Also yes.

## Sunny Shore — Rare Creatures (5)

---

### 16. Moonveil Octopus
**Rarity**: Rare
**Tagline**: *"Extremely intelligent, eight-armed, and almost impossible to catch."*

The Moonveil Octopus appears only on overcast mornings when the light turns silver, emerging briefly from its rock den before disappearing again. It has a pale lavender sheen and shifts to deep blue when startled. Every encounter with one feels like being assessed by something very wise that has simply decided to let you look at it today. It will rearrange any objects you put near its corner of the tank.

---

### 17. Crestfin Seahorse
**Rarity**: Rare
**Tagline**: *"Tiny, majestic, and completely in charge of wherever it happens to be."*

The Crestfin Seahorse has a crown-like crest along its head that catches light like a tiny tiara, which suits its energy perfectly. It grips aquarium plants with its curled tail and bobs thoughtfully in the current, occasionally tilting its head at you with an expression that implies it has given this a great deal of consideration. Rare to discover because it only appears when the tide is very low and the sea is very calm.

---

### 18. Tessellate Ray
**Rarity**: Rare
**Tagline**: *"Glides. Just glides everywhere. Effortlessly and on purpose."*

The Tessellate Ray moves through water the way a shadow moves across the floor: smooth, wide, and very slightly uncanny. Its patterned back — geometric shapes in cream and charcoal — looks like it was tiled by a very talented artist. It's completely harmless and seems deeply unaware that it looks dramatic. Skims just above the tank floor collecting snacks, graceful until it bumps into the glass, at which point it reverses and tries again with the same dignified expression.

---

### 19. Inkwhistle Eel
**Rarity**: Rare
**Tagline**: *"Peeks out of its hiding spot to check if the coast is clear. The coast is never clear enough."*

The Inkwhistle Eel is extremely shy, extremely dramatic about being shy, and extremely beautiful. Dark navy with luminescent pale stripes that run the length of its body, it hides in rock crevices and caves until curiosity gets the better of it, then retreats immediately if anything moves. Players who sit very still near a tide pool at dusk sometimes see one — a long, elegant flash of blue, and then nothing.

---

### 20. Prism Mantis Shrimp
**Rarity**: Rare
**Tagline**: *"Sees sixteen colors. You only see three. It is patient with you about this."*

The Prism Mantis Shrimp is the crown jewel of the Sunny Shore's tide pools: compact, armored, and alive with iridescent color that shifts from emerald to coral to deep violet depending on the angle of light. It is technically the most visually complex creature in the game's first zone, and it carries itself accordingly. Extremely rare to discover. When you do, the discovery animation has an extra flourish, because it has earned it.

## Notes for Art Team (Asset Developer)

- **Creatures 1–8 (Common)**: simple, instantly readable silhouettes — these are the workhorse collection pieces; prioritize charm over complexity
- **Creatures 9–15 (Uncommon)**: opportunity for slightly more elaborate color palettes and surface detail
- **Creatures 16–20 (Rare)**: special shimmer/glow effects justified; discovery animations should feel like a genuine reward
- The Prism Mantis Shrimp (#20) warrants a premium discovery animation — consider a brief "rainbow shimmer" particle burst as part of its reveal

## Notes for Engineer (Spawn Conditions)

- **Moonveil Octopus (#16)**: overcast weather state (cloud cover > 70%)
- **Crestfin Seahorse (#17)**: low-tide window (game time condition: tide state = "low")
- **Inkwhistle Eel (#19)**: dusk time-of-day window (game clock: 17:00–19:00 in-world time)
- **Prism Mantis Shrimp (#20)**: clear weather + midday (high-noon light condition) — to sell the rainbow visual
- All other creatures: standard weighted RNG, no special conditions
