#### Friction Points That Encourage Spending

1. **Growth timers**: Crops take real time to grow. Higher-tier crops take longer, creating natural wait states.
2. **Plot capacity limits**: Starting garden is small (~12 plots). Expansion costs are extreme (250M-1B Sheckles), creating a long grind.
3. **Time-gated expansions**: Back expansions have 1-3 day real-time wait timers, skippable with Robux (199-399 R$).
4. **Weather dependency**: Thunderstorms trigger rare mutations but occur on unpredictable schedules — encourages frequent check-ins.
5. **Season Pass daily engagement**: 250 XP per quest, ~110 days to complete naturally, with 49 R$ tier-skip option creating constant temptation.
6. **Friend boost multiplier** (up to 70x): Social feature that drives invitations and concurrent play.

## 3. Proposed Pearl Economy for Tide Pools

### 3.1 Design Philosophy

Based on the benchmarking, Tide Pools should adopt:
- **Grow a Garden's restraint**: Low monetization aggressiveness, no pay-to-win
- **Fisch's idle layer**: Aquarium passive income as a meaningful secondary loop
- **Pet Sim X's area scaling**: Exponential zone costs with ~4x multiplier per tier
- **All three games' RNG hooks**: Condition-gated rare creatures as engagement drivers

Target monetization aggressiveness: **Low-Medium** (between Grow a Garden and Fisch).

## 4. Summary of Key Recommendations

1. **Match Grow a Garden's restraint, not Pet Sim X's aggression.** Low-medium monetization keeps player sentiment positive and supports long-term retention. GaG's $150M+ with minimal gamepasses proves this works.

2. **The aquarium idle loop is the retention anchor.** Offline pearl production (at 25% of online rate) gives players a reason to return daily. Fisch proved this model works well.

3. **Zone unlock costs should feel achievable but demand commitment.** Rocky Reef in 1-2 hours, The Vents in 1-2 months. The 4x scaling ratio hits the sweet spot.

4. **Creature sell vs. keep is the core economic tension.** Making adult sell prices worth ~8-10 hours of passive income creates genuine decision-making, not just hoarding.

5. **Condition-gated creatures are free engagement mechanics.** Night-only, storm-only, and chain-requirement creatures drive session timing, community discussion, and long-term discovery goals — all without any monetization cost.

6. **Season Pass at 749 R$ (cosmetic-only) is the proven price point.** Grow a Garden validates this exact number. Never put exclusive creatures behind the paywall.

7. **Start conservative, tune up.** It's far easier to increase earn rates or decrease prices post-launch than to nerf an established economy. Ship with the lower end of these ranges and adjust based on playtest data.
