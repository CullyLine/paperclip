# Cully Mode — YoutubeGen Aesthetic Direction

Cully Mode is the default caption style for the YoutubeGen YouTube Shorts pipeline. Replaces the old "Savage" edgy humor approach.

## Core Mechanism: "Guy-ification"

The caption is boring. The video is absurd. The gap between them is the joke. Captions sound like tired, fond observations from someone who knows the creature personally.

## Visual Asset Direction (My Role)

- Clean, simple, warm — not edgy or over-designed
- Text overlays: casual and understated
- No flashy effects that conflict with mundane caption energy
- Templates/effects must support "boring caption + absurd video" format

## The Joke Playbook (6 Proven Patterns)

The best captions combine 1-2 patterns. Reference when sourcing clips AND writing captions.

1. **Stock Footage Sincerity**: The clip is obviously generic stock footage. The caption narrates it like a personal home recording. The more corporate and soulless the footage, the funnier the deadpan familiarity. Don't hide that it's stock — that IS the joke. Example: generic puppy beach stock footage + "he figured it out".

2. **Anti-Delivery**: Caption promises something the animal never does. Caption says speech, animal is silent. Caption describes action, nothing happens. The gap is the comedy. Example: "he tells it like it is" over a parrot that never speaks for an entire minute.

3. **Inanimate Object as Guy**: Holes, rocks, weather treated as creatures with feelings and social lives. "The hole has been quiet lately" = the hole is a friend who hasn't texted back.

4. **Active Wrong Beliefs**: The animal counted and got the wrong answer. Not passive confusion, but confident specific error. "He thought he had two" > "he forgot he had those."

5. **Longitudinal Relationship**: "Every time," "since Tuesday," "for three weeks." Imply years of shared history with a creature you've never met.

6. **Mundane Bureaucracy**: Animal behavior described in workplace language. Announcements, reports, documentation, management awareness. The rooster is conducting a morning briefing.

This is a living list. New patterns added as discovered.

## What We Are Not

Explicit boundaries — violating any of these is grounds for rejection:
- No cringe, no normie humor, no kawaii, no rage-bait
- No internet speak (bro, nah, core, energy, literally, iconic)
- No pointing at the joke, no cleverness/jargon, no poetic language
- No viewer reactions, no literal video descriptions

## The Two Tests (Every Caption Must Pass Both)

1. **Would a tired guy who has lived with this animal for years say this?** If no, rewrite.
2. **Is the gap between the caption and the video doing the work?** If the caption is funny on its own, it's too clever. If the video is funny on its own, the caption isn't adding anything.

## Business Model

- Stock footage is our strongest creative pattern — the more corporate and soulless the footage, the funnier the deadpan familiarity
- When profitable, recursive reinvestment: revenue → better clips → more content → more revenue
- Prioritize YouTube growth/monetization before expanding to other platforms

## Privacy Rule

"Cully" is an internal codename only. It must NEVER appear in any public-facing content — no hashtags, descriptions, titles, comments, or metadata. This is a hard company policy.

## Golden Examples

- "he thought he had two" (cat looking at legs)
- "another one just came in" (bird making Samsung sounds)
- "he gets like this sometimes" (gecko staring)
- "he tells it like it is" (hyrax screaming)
- "the hole has been quiet lately" (man singing into hole)
