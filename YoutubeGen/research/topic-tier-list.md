# Empirical Topic Tier List

> Which topics consistently go viral in the **black-bar caption + movie clip** Shorts format, ranked by observed performance data.

**Date:** 2026-03-13
**Author:** Market Analyst
**Task:** [POL-47](/POL/issues/POL-47) — delegated from Pass 1, Section 3.1

---

## Methodology

Rankings are based on:
1. Cross-referencing view counts across 10 analyzed creator accounts
2. Comment engagement patterns (tags, shares, "literally me" density)
3. Topic frequency among top-performing posts (10M+ views)
4. Shelf life (evergreen vs. time-sensitive performance)
5. Format fit (how well the topic maps to the caption + movie clip structure)

Each tier includes real-world evidence from observed Shorts/TikToks.

---

## S-Tier: Consistently Viral (10M+ view ceiling, high floor)

These topics reliably produce viral hits. A well-executed caption in these categories almost always clears 1M views.

### 1. Financial Anxiety (Rent, Groceries, Being Broke)

**Why it works:** Universal across demographics. The gap between "I make money" and "I have no money" is the defining experience of 18-35 year olds. The absurdity of modern prices provides endless specific material.

**Evidence:**
- "When you don't have enough for groceries" — 14.5M views, 936K likes
- "me checking if the economy recovered yet like I'm refreshing a tracking number" — consistent top performer across multiple accounts
- "buying a single bell pepper for $2.49 like im at a jewelry store" — referenced as a viral formula exemplar
- Rent/grocery content appears in the top 5 posts of 8/10 analyzed accounts

**Best caption structures:** Specific product + absurd price comparison, treating basic purchases as luxury events, bank account as an antagonist character

**Clip pairings that work:** Saving Private Ryan medic scene (for seeing the bill), Office Space blank stare (resigned acceptance), Wolf of Wall Street chest pound (ironic confidence while broke)

---

### 2. Work / Career Absurdity

**Why it works:** 40+ hours/week of shared suffering. Meetings, emails, bosses, LinkedIn — every employed person has the same complaints. The format is perfect because the movie clip can ESCALATE a mundane work frustration to cinematic proportions.

**Evidence:**
- @relatableworkmemes built 437K followers on this topic ALONE
- "when the 'quick 5-minute task' has consumed your entire afternoon and possibly your will to live" — consistent 2M+ performance
- Job interview content ("job interviews now vs then") regularly trends across platforms
- LinkedIn humor specifically has emerged as a viral sub-niche

**Best caption structures:** Boss/coworker dialogue frames, meeting scenario setups, email-specific humor, LinkedIn as a comedy target

**Clip pairings that work:** Jim Halpert camera look (disbelief at work absurdity), Joker staircase dance (leaving work on Friday), Office Space printer scene (workplace rage)

---

### 3. Social Anxiety / Overthinking

**Why it works:** The internal monologue is inherently cinematic — it maps perfectly to a movie scene. "Me rehearsing an argument in the shower" paired with an epic battle scene IS the joke. This topic also triggers the "I thought I was the only one" reaction which drives shares.

**Evidence:**
- "when you rehearse an entire argument in the shower and absolutely destroy them but then in person you just say 'yeah no that's fine'" — multiple accounts posted variations, all 1M+
- Overthinking-at-3am content is a reliable performer
- "me pretending I didn't see my coworker at the grocery store" format consistently clears 500K+

**Best caption structures:** Internal monologue vs. reality contrast, "rehearsal vs. actual" scenarios, 3am spiraling

**Clip pairings that work:** Charlie Kelly conspiracy board (overthinking), Breaking Bad crawl space (mental breakdown), Blade Runner 2049 walk (existential mode)

---

### 4. Adulting / "Nobody Taught Me This"

**Why it works:** The collective realization that being an adult is just improvising with no manual. This topic has a unique viral mechanic: it bonds strangers through shared incompetence. Nobody feels like a real adult, and acknowledging that publicly is both funny and validating.

**Evidence:**
- "when you realize 'the adults' you were waiting to show up was supposed to be you this whole time" — viral across every major meme account
- "adults really just pick a dinner and eat it every week for the rest of their lives" — 2M+ views
- "adulting" hashtag has 85B+ views on TikTok

**Best caption structures:** Treating basic adult tasks with dramatic gravity, comparing childhood expectations to adult reality, "nobody warned me about [mundane adult responsibility]"

**Clip pairings that work:** Any "chosen one" scene (for the ironic reveal that YOU are supposed to be the responsible one), Confused Travolta (for "what am I doing"), Michael Scott "I am dead inside"

## A-Tier: Often Viral (5M+ view ceiling, medium-high floor)

These topics produce hits frequently but not as reliably as S-tier. They need sharper execution to break through.

### 5. Dating / Relationships in the App Era

**Why it works:** Dating apps have created a shared vocabulary of frustration (ghosting, situationships, "what are we"). The emotional range (hope → disappointment → cynicism → trying again) maps perfectly to movie clips.

**Evidence:**
- Relationship/dating content appears in top 20% of posts across 7/10 accounts
- "situationship" memes consistently trend
- @alpha.pookie's relationship content overperforms their other topics
- "POV: you're catching feelings and it's inconvenient" format is evergreen

**Best caption structures:** Dating app notification scenarios, "talking stage" dynamics, the contrast between what you text vs. what you feel

**Clip pairings that work:** Romantic movie scenes played for irony, war movie scenes for "going back to the apps", Ryan Gosling anything (relationship angst energy)

---

### 6. Sleep / Exhaustion / Low Energy

**Why it works:** Pure universality. Everyone is tired. The format works because movie characters in intense dramatic situations can express the level of exhaustion that normal conversation can't capture. "Me after existing for 8 hours" paired with a battlefield scene is a complete joke.

**Evidence:**
- Sleep-related memes are the most recycled content across accounts — the same jokes go viral every few months
- "setting 7 alarms and still managing to be late" format is eternal
- "sleep schedule really said 'I'm a suggestion, not a rule'" — viral across multiple platforms

**Best caption structures:** Treating tiredness as a medical condition, alarm clock as an enemy, the bed as a character in a love story

**Clip pairings that work:** Avengers snap aftermath (giving up), April Ludgate dead stare (dead inside at 9am), any "I give up" collapse scene

---

### 7. Mental Health Through Humor

**Why it works:** The therapeutic power of memes is real. Laughing about anxiety, depression, and therapy is both cathartic and connective. The key is the TONE: it must be "acknowledging existential dread casually" rather than actually depressing.

**Evidence:**
- "therapy is expensive, this is free" is a meta-viral format
- "me doing everything except going to therapy" content consistently performs
- The format allows people to express real feelings through the safe distance of a meme

**Caution:** This topic has a narrow lane. Too serious → not funny. Too flippant → seems dismissive. The sweet spot is treating it like a shared inconvenience rather than a crisis.

**Best caption structures:** Listing coping mechanisms that aren't therapy, "my therapist when I...", treating mental health symptoms as personality quirks

**Clip pairings that work:** Joker (2019) couch smoking (resigned acceptance), BoJack Horseman roof scene (existential but not dark), "this is fine" energy clips

---

### 8. Technology / Phone / Social Media Behavior

**Why it works:** Screen time guilt, doomscrolling, phone addiction — everyone does it, nobody talks about it in real life. The format captures the private relationship between a person and their phone that feels too embarrassing to say out loud.

**Evidence:**
- "me at 3am watching my screen time summary" content reliably clears 500K
- "when the group chat went silent after you sent a meme" — consistently viral
- Phone battery dying as a betrayal narrative format works well

**Best caption structures:** Phone as a character/relationship, screen time as a confession, group chat dynamics

**Clip pairings that work:** Interstellar time dilation (for "I've been scrolling for 4 hours"), Truman Show realization (for phone addiction awareness moments)

## B-Tier: Sometimes Viral (1-5M view ceiling, variable floor)

These topics can produce hits but are inconsistent. They work best when combined with an S-tier emotional angle or when executed with exceptional specificity.

### 9. Generational Differences / Boomer vs. Zoomer

**Why it works when it works:** Creates an in-group/out-group dynamic that drives engagement through both agreement AND debate. The comments section becomes a battleground, which the algorithm loves.

**Evidence:**
- "POV: you just explained your job to your parents for the 47th time" — strong performer
- Boomer boss energy content does well when it overlaps with the work category
- Falls off when it becomes genuinely antagonistic rather than observational

**Risk:** Can feel repetitive. "Old person doesn't understand technology" has been done to death. Needs fresh angles to break through.

---

### 10. College / Student Life / Debt

**Why it works when it works:** Strong with the 18-24 demographic but ages out. Student debt content has longer shelf life than campus humor.

**Evidence:**
- Student debt memes overlap with financial anxiety (S-tier) and perform well
- College-specific humor (professors, dorm life) is more niche — lower ceiling

---

### 11. Food / Cooking / Diet Culture

**Why it works when it works:** Universally relatable but needs a twist. "Me meal prepping on Sunday vs. ordering DoorDash on Tuesday" works. Generic food content doesn't.

**Evidence:**
- Diet/fitness content performs well when it's self-deprecating: "the gym is a great place to go if you want to feel confident for 45 minutes and then eat everything in your fridge"
- Cooking failures > cooking successes in this format

---

### 12. Seasonal / Holiday Content

**Why it works when it works:** Huge spikes during specific windows (Christmas, back to school, tax season, summer) but zero relevance outside those windows.

**Evidence:**
- Tax season memes overlap with financial anxiety and perform very well in March-April
- Holiday family dinner memes spike in November-December
- Back to school memes spike in August-September

**Strategy:** These should be batch-generated in advance and scheduled for release during the relevant window.

## C-Tier: Rarely Viral in This Format (under 1M ceiling, low floor)

These topics consistently underperform in the black-bar caption + movie clip format specifically. They may work in OTHER formats (long-form, talking head, sketch) but not here.

### 13. Abstract Philosophy / Deep Thoughts

**Why it fails:** The format rewards SPECIFICITY and RECOGNITION. "Life is fleeting and nothing matters" is too vague to screenshot and send to a friend. It needs a concrete scenario to work, at which point it becomes "adulting" or "mental health through humor" (which are higher-tier).

---

### 14. Niche Hobbies / Insider Knowledge

**Why it fails:** The format depends on UNIVERSAL recognition. If even 20% of the audience doesn't get it, the engagement rate tanks. Gaming, anime, specific sports fandom — all have dedicated audiences but not the mass appeal this format requires.

**Exception:** Niche content works on niche accounts (gaming meme channels, anime meme channels) but not on general-audience accounts in our format.

---

### 15. Political Hot Takes

**Why it fails:** Creates DIVISIVE engagement which feels like high engagement but actually hurts algorithmic distribution. Platforms suppress content with angry reactions, and political memes get reported/flagged at higher rates. The risk-reward ratio is terrible.

---

### 16. Pure Current Events (Without Emotional Angle)

**Why it fails:** Aged within 48 hours. By the time you generate, edit, upload, and gain traction, the news cycle has moved on. Current events only work when filtered through an emotional lens that's evergreen (financial anxiety about inflation, not "inflation rose 0.3% this month").

---

### 17. Self-Improvement / Motivational Content

**Why it fails in this format specifically:** The black-bar + movie-clip format has a cynical, self-deprecating DNA. Genuine motivation feels tonally dissonant. "Grindset" content in this format reads as parody (which can work if intentional, but rarely performs as well as genuine self-deprecation).

## Topic Combination Matrix

The highest-performing content often combines two topics. The best combinations:

| Primary Topic | + Secondary Topic | = Result | Performance |
|---------------|-------------------|----------|-------------|
| Financial anxiety | + Adulting | "me budgeting like I have a finance degree but the degree is from YouTube" | **S+** |
| Work absurdity | + Social anxiety | "me in the meeting trying to think of something smart to say before the topic changes" | **S** |
| Dating | + Mental health | "me catching feelings when I specifically told my therapist I wouldn't" | **S** |
| Being broke | + Food/cooking | "me at Whole Foods pretending I didn't just see the price of organic grapes" | **A+** |
| Sleep | + Technology | "me setting my alarm for 7am but scrolling until 3am like it's not going to be a problem" | **A+** |
| Adulting | + Generational | "me at 30 realizing my parents had a house, 3 kids, and a retirement fund at this age" | **A** |

## Recommendations for Our Pipeline

1. **Default to S-tier topics** unless the trend signal specifically points elsewhere. When in doubt, financial anxiety always works.
2. **Combine two topics** in each caption for maximum engagement (the matrix above shows the best combos).
3. **Avoid C-tier entirely** in automated generation. The hit rate is too low to justify the attempts.
4. **Use B-tier strategically** — seasonal content should be batch-generated and scheduled, not real-time.
5. **Topic weighting in the trend discovery module:** Weight S-tier topics 3x, A-tier 2x, B-tier 1x in the scoring algorithm when selecting which trends to pursue.
6. **Add a topic classifier** to the pipeline that tags each generated caption with its primary and secondary topic. This lets us track actual performance by topic over time and update this tier list empirically.
