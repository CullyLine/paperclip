# Caption Style Guide
## YouTube Shorts Generator — Zoomer Humor Edition

---

## Target Voice

**Age:** ~27-year-old zoomers (born ~1997–2001)
**Tone:** Post-ironic, self-deprecating, relatable, sometimes nihilistic
**Energy:** "Me when" — dry, exhausted, emotionally aware but pretending not to care
**Core emotion:** Quiet suffering with a smirk. The joke lands because it's *true*, not because it tries hard.

---

## The Vibe in One Sentence

> *"We know everything is broken and we're fine with it, mostly."*

This is not boomer cynicism (angry) or millennial sarcasm (over-explained). It's zoomer acceptance — the humor of someone who has fully processed the absurdity and moved on to just... posting about it.

---

## Tone Rules

### Do This
- Be **specific** — "paying rent, student loans, and one (1) avocado" beats "spending money"
- Use **understatement** — the worse the situation, the calmer the caption
- Embrace **self-awareness** — acknowledge you're doing the bit
- Reference **mundane horrors** — dentist appointments, emails, LinkedIn notifications
- Use **numbers for comedic specificity** — "my 47th rewatch," "6-8 business years"
- Let the **movie clip carry the emotion** — the caption sets up, the clip delivers
- Write like you're **texting a friend at 2am**, not performing

### Don't Do This
- **Over-explain the joke** — if you have to explain it, kill it
- Use **dated references** (anything that requires "remember when?")
- Be **mean-spirited** toward specific groups — self-deprecation only
- Try **too hard** to be relatable — "fellow kids" energy kills it instantly
- Use **exclamation points** — exhaustion is the brand
- Write **long captions** — if it doesn't fit in the black bar at readable size, it's too long
- Use **caps lock for emphasis** — italics energy, not SHOUTING energy
- Do **cringe positivity spins** — "at least we're in it together!" No.

## Caption Structure Patterns

### 1. "me when" / "me watching"
The classic. Observational. Present tense. No subject needed.

> `me watching my savings account after paying rent`
> `me when my brain decides 3am is the perfect time to replay that embarrassing thing from 2014`
> `me watching my coworker genuinely enjoy a team-building activity`

### 2. "POV:"
Sets a scene. Makes the viewer the protagonist.

> `POV: you're explaining your job to your parents for the 47th time`
> `POV: you opened LinkedIn by accident and now you're spiraling`
> `POV: it's Sunday night`

### 3. "when you..."
Second person. Slightly more conversational.

> `when you realize "figuring it out" was supposed to have an end date`
> `when you've been "almost done" with that one task for 3 hours`
> `when the work event is "mandatory fun"`

### 4. "nobody: / me:"
The nobody/somebody format. Sets up contrast.

> `nobody: / me at 2am: [something unhinged but relatable]`
> `nobody: / my anxiety:`

### 5. Standalone observation (no prefix)
Just a statement. Confident. Dry.

> `adulting is just paying bills until you die, occasionally`
> `the audacity of a Monday after a good weekend`
> `"self-care" but make it lying on the floor not in a sad way`

### 6. The "actually" subversion
Sets up an expectation, then undercuts it.

> `turns out "growth mindset" means accepting you'll feel like this forever`
> `therapist said to challenge negative thoughts. she didn't say they'd win`

## Length Guidelines

- **Hard max:** 12 words for a single-line caption at comfortable reading size in the black bar
- **Two lines:** Up to 8 words per line — must be intentionally broken, not wrapped randomly
- **Sweet spot:** 6–10 words total
- **Font size:** Large enough to read on mobile without squinting (roughly 60–80px equivalent at 1080px wide)
- **Test it:** If you have to shrink the font to fit, rewrite the caption — don't shrink the font

**Good (fits):**
> `me watching my bank account do its thing` (8 words)

**Too long:**
> `me watching my savings account after paying rent student loans and buying a single avocado at whole foods` (rewrite needed)

**Two-line example:**
> `POV: it's sunday night`
> `and monday already won`

## What Makes It Land vs. Cringe

### Why It Lands
| Element | What It Does |
|---|---|
| Specificity | "one (1) grocery" > "groceries" — the specificity IS the joke |
| Understatement | The calmer the caption, the harder the clip hit |
| Earned nihilism | Sounds like someone who's processed the suffering, not wallowing |
| No punchline announcement | The caption doesn't say "lol" or "right??" — it trusts the reader |
| Present-tense immediacy | Feels like it's happening right now |
| The clip recontextualizes | Caption is dry, clip is dramatic — the contrast is the comedy |

### Why It's Cringe
| Element | What Goes Wrong |
|---|---|
| Over-trying | "Who else can relate? 😂😭" — desperation energy |
| Wrong specificity | "my boss named Karen" — too targeted, too sitcom |
| Forced Gen Z slang | "no cap this slaps fr fr" — sounds like a brand account |
| Boomer repackaged | Complaining about kids/phones — wrong audience |
| Positive twist ending | "but honestly, we got this!" — betrays the bit |
| Explaining the vibe | "...because adulting is hard" — don't state the thesis |

## Mood Tags Reference

Use these when annotating captions for the Scene Matcher module:

- `defeated` — resigned, done, gave up gracefully
- `smug` — knows something others don't, quiet superiority
- `shocked` — the "wait what" face
- `resigned` — accepted the bad outcome, moving on
- `dissociating` — checked out, somewhere else entirely
- `spiraling` — controlled chaos, aware it's happening
- `unbothered` — choosing peace through willful ignorance
- `vindicated` — "I knew it"
- `exhausted` — not sad, just tired
- `darkly-amused` — laughing at the void, somewhat
